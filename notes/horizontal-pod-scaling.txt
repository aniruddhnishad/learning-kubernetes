HPA — Horizontal Pod Autoscaler

This is where Kubernetes becomes truly “cloud-native”.

HPA automatically:

increases pods during traffic spikes
decreases pods when traffic drops

################################################
Suppose your app has:

2 pods

Suddenly:

 - traffic increases
 - CPU becomes 95%

Users experience:

 - slow response
 - timeouts
 - crashes

Manually scaling is bad.

🚀 HPA SOLUTION

Kubernetes watches metrics:

 - CPU
 - memory
 - custom metrics

Then automatically:

High load
   ↓
increase replicas

Low load
   ↓
decrease replicas

###########################
🚀 EXAMPLE

Without HPA:

2 pods forever

With HPA:

2 pods → 5 pods → 10 pods

depending on traffic.

######################################

VERY IMPORTANT CONCEPT

HPA scales Deployments not individual pods.

###############################################

REQUIREMENTS

Before HPA:

 - metrics-server installed
 - deployment has CPU requests

VERY important:
 - HPA uses: requests to calculate utilization.

 INTERVIEW QUESTIONS FROM HPA

Very common:

❓ What does HPA scale?

 - Deployments/ReplicaSets.

❓ Does HPA create nodes?

❌ No.

That is: Cluster Autoscaler

❓ What metrics can HPA use?
 - CPU
 - memory
 - custom metrics
